  • Baghali Polo

    (Veg/ V/ N/ Chicken/Fish/Lamb)
    price: 149kr

    Baghali polo is a popular Iranian dish of rice, fava beans and dill. In Persian, baghali means fava bean while polo is pilaf, a style of cooked rice. It is made by cooking rice and green broad beans in boiling water. This dish can be served in all varieties.

  • Zereshk Polo ba Morgh

    (N/ Chicken)
    price: 109kr

    Zereshk Polo ba Morgh is a Persian classic made with Zereshk (barberries), Morgh (chicken), and Polo, which is the steamed and fluffy Persian rice. This is one of the mixed rice dishes that you will find at small casual get togethers, as well as larger Persian dinner parties, weddings and most festive celebrations and holidays.

  • Khoreshte Gheime Bademjan

    (Veg/ V/Lamb)
    price: 109kr

    Lamb Aubergine Stew with Yellow Split Peas gives you an idea of the main ingredients of this dish. Khoresht is a general Persian name for stews, while Gheymeh refers to the particular size of the meat used in this stew. Bademjan means eggplant in Persian. Most of the Persian stews are served over Persian steamed rice, so you can eat this saffron-flavored stew along with Persian rice..

  • Khoreshte Ghorme Sabzi

    (Veg/ V/Lamb)
    price: 109kr

    The main ingredients are a mixture of sauteed herbs, consisting mainly of parsley, leeks and coriander, seasoned with the key spice of dried fenugreek leaves.This mixture is cooked with kidney beans, black lime, and turmeric-seasoned lamb. Vegetarian ghormeh sabzi may substitute whole walnut pieces or mushrooms for the meat.

  • Khoreste Fesenjoon ba Morgh

    (Veg/ V/ N/ Chicken)
    price: 109kr

    Fesenjoon is an Iranian stew (a khoresh) from Gilan province, Iran. It is flavored with pomegranate paste and ground walnuts, and spices like turmeric, cinnamon, cardamom, and rosebud. It is traditionally made with poultry chicken.
